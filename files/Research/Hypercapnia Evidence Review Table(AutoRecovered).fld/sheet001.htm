Ouanes-Besbes et al. 2021, Tunisia What is the frequency of undiagnosed sleep apnea among patients admitted to the ICU with hypercapnic respiratory failure? Admitted to ICU 2015-2018, PaCO2 > 6kpa, pH < 7.35. No prior OSA syndrome diagnosis. 104 of 124 who undersent HSAT had severe OSA. 34 were low risk by STOP-BANG (<=3 at ICU discharge). 40 declined HSAT or lost to followup. Thus, overall prevalence in survivors was over 50% Tunisia 80% obese, 63% smoking, 48% COPD, 23% OHS

Patients starting home volume-cycled ventilators (excluding ASV) for 3+ months (by voluntary participation of pulmonologists, LTACs, hospitals in the area) NIV use tripled since 200. Med. Exclude NM dz, iatrogenic, or with persisting confusion 21% had been hospitalized in the last yr for resp failure. 67% had COPD. 81 percent of patients without COPD were obese. 29% had OSA known (mostly untreated); 66% of those tested had mod-severe OSA. 51% had COPD&OSA. 44% had HFpEF, ~16%ish had pHTN -unclear selection pressures from exclusion criteria (50% excluded) Patients who were not treated for OSA had higher readmissions (similar with others)
